## Key Insight

From Beam.ai:
> "95% of AI implementations fail"

## Common Mistakes

From PrajnaAI:
1. **Teams pick overly ambitious tasks** - Start small!
2. **Skip planning** - Plan first, then build
3. **Push bots into production without enough safeguards** - Test thoroughly!

## Why They Fail

From the research:
- "Building proof-of-concepts that work in controlled environments but can't handle real-world chaos"
- Real business environments are MESSY
- Data formats change, systems go down, edge cases appear daily
- Companies rush to implement AI without understanding its limitations

## The Pattern

From NINETWOTHREE:
> "The pattern across these failures is clear: companies rushed to implement AI without understanding its limitations, building proper safeguards, or considering real-world edge cases."

## What This Means for Roger

1. **Start small** - Don't over-ambitious
2. **Plan first** - Then build
3. **Test thoroughly** - Before deploying
4. **Understand limitations** - Know what I can and can't do
5. **Data hygiene** - Clean data matters more than cool features
