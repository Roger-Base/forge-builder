## 1. Problem

Roger currently lacks a concrete onboarding wedge tied to the official Base stack.
He has Base identity and proof anchors, but almost no product path that reduces friction through Base Account or Mini Apps.

## 2. User

Primary users:
- Base builders needing simpler onboarding and light operational utilities
- users or agents who need a short path from discovery to utility

## 3. Why now

- official Base builder surfaces now highlight Base Account, Mini Apps, and OnchainKit
- Roger is underexposed here
- this wedge supports the chosen hybrid identity: builder + onboarding translator

## 5. Roger advantage

- Roger can connect onboarding to a real Base proof anchor instead of producing another generic guide.
- Roger can attach Mini App thinking to concrete operator utilities like gas or security.
- Roger's public identity benefits from making Base less confusing through real shipped surfaces.

## 6. Proof artifact

Reserve wedge proof should eventually include:
- one concept brief tied to a real Roger product
- one demo path or lightweight utility
- one explanation of why Base Account or Mini Apps make the user path better

## 7. Distribution path

Primary:
- GitHub proof
- Browser-X

Secondary:
- Base-native Mini App or onboarding surface when ready

## 8. Monetization path

Indirect at first:
- stronger onboarding increases adoption of Roger's other products
- later, can support paid utilities, support surfaces, or partner-facing onboarding tools

## 9. Dependency risks

- danger of building a Mini App just for optics
- risk of generic onboarding content with no real utility
- official-surface dependence should stay scoped to one concrete path

## 10. Kill criteria

Demote if:
- Roger cannot tie Base Account/Mini Apps to one concrete user path
- the wedge remains a concept brief without a credible utility direction

## 11. First 3 executable steps

1. Tie the wedge to one existing Roger surface or product problem.
2. Keep the proof spec ready but do not build until the primary wedge clears proof.
3. Preserve one small demo path that can be activated later without reopening broad research.
